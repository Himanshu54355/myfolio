Most recent projects and Certifications

Modular Drone

The project involved designing a modular, ready-to-assemble drone with replaceable CFRP parts for multipurpose use. Modeled in Fusion 360 and analyzed in ANSYS 2022 R2, the drone supports both 6-axis and lightweight 4-axis configurations. Structural and fluent analyses assessed durability and aerodynamics, with lift and drag coefficients used to estimate load capacity for both setups.

Vibration Monitoring

Root Cause analysis of Regeneration Blower at DTA-HNUU aromatics site at Reliance Industries Ltd. using Vibration monitoring technique and suggesting measures to limit vibrations and prevent premature bearing failure. This experience enhanced my understanding of reliability engineering and real-time diagnostics in large-scale industrial operations.

Centrifugal Pump CFD Simulation

The project involved simulating water flow through a centrifugal pump. The pump geometry (impeller, volute, inlet/outlet) was modeled in Fusion 360 and imported into SimScale. Using the Navier-Stokes equations, the simulation analyzed pressure distribution, velocity profiles, and turbulence to evaluate hydraulic performance.

Analysis of Cooling Systems for battery pack

The project involved modeling a battery pack in ANSYS 2022 R2 and performing transient thermal and fluid analysis using air, water, and ethylene glycol as cooling media. The study compared their cooling performance based on heat absorption and temperature differences using velocity and temperature graphs and contours.

Analysis of Inconel 718 based SLM printed Brake Disc

The project compared stainless-steel and Inconel 718 brake discs, with and without a new slotted groove design, using Selective Laser Melting. A 2×2 matrix analysis was performed with structural, thermal, modal, and frictional tests. Inconel showed better stress and heat performance, while the slotted design improved heat dissipation but increased vibration susceptibility.

Ceramic Matrix Composite (CMC)

The project focused on developing a SiC/ZrO₂ ceramic matrix composite (CMC). Composite powders were mixed, pressed, and sintered at 1100°C to achieve dense microstructures. Mechanical properties were assessed via compressive strength and hardness tests, while optical microscopy revealed uniform reinforcement distribution. Results showed promising performance for high-temperature applications.
